# Application Rationalization Project Plan

## Client Engagement Template

---

## Executive Summary

This project plan outlines a comprehensive approach to evaluate, analyze, and rationalize a client's application portfolio. The engagement will identify opportunities for cost reduction, risk mitigation, and strategic alignment while providing a clear roadmap for portfolio optimization.

**Typical Duration:** 8-12 weeks (depending on portfolio size)
**Key Deliverables:** Portfolio assessment, stakeholder insights, rationalization recommendations, implementation roadmap

---

## Phase 2: Data Collection & Inventory

### Duration: Week 2-4

### Objectives
- Build comprehensive application inventory
- Collect quantitative data on all applications
- Validate data accuracy with application owners
- Establish baseline metrics

### Activities

| Activity | Owner | Duration | Deliverable |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Distribute data collection templates to IT teams | Analyst | Day 1 | Template distribution |
| Collect application inventory data | Analyst | Days 1-10 | Raw inventory data |
| Gather cost data (licensing, infrastructure, support) | Analyst | Days 3-10 | Cost inventory |
| Extract technical metrics (usage, performance, integrations) | Technical Lead | Days 5-12 | Technical metrics |
| Compile vendor/contract information | Analyst | Days 5-10 | Vendor registry |
| Data validation and cleansing | Analyst | Days 10-14 | Validated dataset |
| Import data into Rationalization Tool | Technical Lead | Day 14 | Loaded tool |

### Data Points to Collect

**Core Application Data:**
- Application name and description
- Business owner and IT owner
- Department/business unit
- User count (active users)
- Deployment model (on-prem, cloud, SaaS, hybrid)

**Financial Data:**
- Annual licensing costs
- Infrastructure costs (hosting, storage)
- Support and maintenance costs
- Development/enhancement costs
- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

**Technical Data:**
- Technology stack (languages, frameworks, databases)
- Age of application
- Last major update
- Integration points (upstream/downstream)
- Vendor support status
- Security vulnerabilities
- Compliance requirements

**Business Data:**
- Business criticality rating
- Revenue impact
- Regulatory requirements
- Strategic alignment score
- Redundancy with other applications

### Data Sources
- CMDB/Asset Management Systems
- Financial systems (cost allocation)
- IT Service Management tools
- Application Performance Monitoring
- Contract management systems
- Security scanning tools
- Direct surveys to application owners

## Phase 3: Stakeholder Interviews

### Duration: Week 3-5 (overlaps with Phase 2)

### Objectives
- Capture qualitative insights from key stakeholders
- Understand business context and pain points
- Assess change readiness and political landscape
- Identify hidden dependencies and tribal knowledge

### Activities

| Activity | Owner | Duration | Deliverable |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Schedule interviews (30-60 min each) | Project Coordinator | Days 1-3 | Interview calendar |
| Prepare interview guides per stakeholder type | Analyst | Days 1-3 | Customized questionnaires |
| Conduct executive interviews | Project Lead | Days 4-12 | Executive insights |
| Conduct business owner interviews | Analyst | Days 4-14 | Business perspectives |
| Conduct IT/technical interviews | Technical Lead | Days 7-14 | Technical insights |
| Conduct end-user interviews | Analyst | Days 10-14 | User feedback |
| Enter responses into Stakeholder Assessment Tool | Analyst | Ongoing | Captured responses |
| Analyze interview results | Analyst | Days 14-17 | Interview analysis report |

### Interview Strategy

**Executive Sponsors (2-3 interviews)**
- Strategic priorities and constraints
- Budget considerations
- Risk tolerance
- Timeline expectations
- Success metrics

**Business Owners (5-10 interviews)**
- Business value and criticality
- Future needs and gaps
- Pain points and frustrations
- Change readiness
- Dependencies on other systems

**IT Leaders (3-5 interviews)**
- Technical health and sustainability
- Support burden
- Integration complexity
- Security and compliance concerns
- Modernization opportunities

**Power Users (5-10 interviews)**
- Daily usage patterns
- Satisfaction and usability
- Workarounds and manual processes
- Feature gaps
- Training and support quality

### Interview Categories (from Stakeholder Assessment Tool)

1. **Business Value & Criticality** - Strategic importance, revenue impact
2. **User Satisfaction & Experience** - Usability, reliability, support quality
3. **Technical Health & Sustainability** - Integration, data quality, mobile access
4. **Change Readiness & Migration Potential** - Openness to change, training effort
5. **Dependencies & Integration** - Upstream/downstream systems, manual bridges
6. **Cost & Resource Awareness** - Value perception, support burden, duplicates
7. **Future Needs & Strategic Alignment** - Capability gaps, digital transformation fit

### Deliverables
- [ ] Completed Interview Assessments (in tool)
- [ ] Stakeholder Sentiment Analysis
- [ ] Key Themes and Patterns Report
- [ ] Risk and Opportunity Register
- [ ] Verbatim Quotes Document

### Success Criteria
- Minimum 15-20 interviews completed
- All critical applications covered
- Executive, business, and technical perspectives captured
- Clear patterns and themes identified

## Phase 4: Assessment & Analysis

### Duration: Week 5-7

### Objectives
- Calculate composite scores for all applications
- Apply TIME framework categorization
- Identify patterns, clusters, and anomalies
- Generate compliance and risk assessments
- Develop data-driven insights

### Activities

| Activity | Owner | Duration | Deliverable |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Run scoring engine on portfolio | Technical Lead | Day 1 | Composite scores |
| Apply TIME framework categorization | Technical Lead | Day 1 | TIME quadrant placement |
| Run ML clustering analysis | Technical Lead | Day 2 | Application clusters |
| Identify anomalies and outliers | Analyst | Days 2-3 | Anomaly report |
| Conduct compliance assessment | Analyst | Days 3-5 | Compliance scores |
| Perform risk assessment | Analyst | Days 4-6 | Risk matrix |
| Analyze dependencies and integration map | Technical Lead | Days 5-7 | Dependency visualization |
| Correlate stakeholder scores with technical data | Analyst | Days 6-8 | Correlation analysis |
| Identify consolidation opportunities | Analyst | Days 7-9 | Consolidation candidates |
| Generate smart recommendations | Technical Lead | Day 9 | Recommendations list |
| What-if scenario modeling | Analyst | Days 9-10 | Scenario analysis |

### Analysis Techniques

**Quantitative Analysis:**
- Composite scoring (weighted multi-criteria)
- TIME framework (Tolerate/Invest/Migrate/Eliminate)
- Total Cost of Ownership calculations
- Usage vs. cost efficiency analysis
- Technical debt assessment
- Redundancy identification

**Qualitative Analysis:**
- Stakeholder sentiment aggregation
- Pain point frequency analysis
- Change readiness heat mapping
- Dependency complexity scoring
- Strategic alignment assessment

**Advanced Analytics:**
- ML clustering for natural application groupings
- Anomaly detection for outliers
- Predictive modeling for future state
- Scenario comparison modeling

### TIME Framework Definitions

| Quadrant | Business Value | Technical Quality | Action |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Invest** | High | High | Enhance and grow |
| **Tolerate** | High | Low | Plan modernization |
| **Migrate** | Low | High | Consolidate or replace |
| **Eliminate** | Low | Low | Retire or decommission |

## Phase 5: Recommendations Development

### Duration: Week 7-9

### Objectives
- Develop specific recommendations for each application
- Quantify benefits (cost savings, risk reduction)
- Define implementation approach
- Prioritize actions based on value and feasibility
- Build business cases for key initiatives

### Activities

| Activity | Owner | Duration | Deliverable |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Develop recommendations per application | Analyst | Days 1-5 | Recommendation register |
| Estimate cost savings per recommendation | Analyst | Days 3-7 | Cost savings model |
| Assess implementation complexity | Technical Lead | Days 4-7 | Complexity ratings |
| Calculate ROI for major initiatives | Analyst | Days 6-9 | Business cases |
| Prioritize recommendations (value vs. effort) | Project Lead | Days 8-10 | Prioritized list |
| Validate recommendations with client SMEs | Project Lead | Days 10-12 | Validated recommendations |
| Refine based on feedback | Analyst | Days 12-14 | Final recommendations |

### Recommendation Categories

**Retire/Eliminate:**
- Applications with low value and high cost
- Redundant systems with better alternatives
- End-of-life or unsupported systems
- Unused or underutilized applications

**Consolidate:**
- Overlapping functionality across multiple apps
- Multiple instances of same application
- Department-specific tools that could be enterprise-wide
- Point solutions that could join platforms

**Migrate/Modernize:**
- Legacy systems with high business value
- On-prem applications suited for cloud
- Monolithic apps to microservices
- Outdated technology stacks

**Invest/Enhance:**
- Strategic applications needing new features
- High-value systems requiring performance improvements
- Applications central to digital transformation

**Maintain/Tolerate:**
- Stable applications meeting current needs
- Systems with planned future replacement
- Applications with high switching costs

### Business Case Components
- Current state costs (annual)
- Future state costs (projected)
- One-time implementation costs
- Net savings over 3-5 years
- Risk reduction benefits
- Strategic alignment benefits
- Implementation timeline
- Dependencies and prerequisites

### Success Criteria

- Quantified savings for retire/consolidate candidates
- Business cases for major initiatives

- Clear prioritization criteria

### Duration: Week 9-10

### Objectives
- Develop phased implementation roadmap
- Sequence initiatives based on dependencies
- Balance quick wins with strategic initiatives
- Align with budget cycles and resource availability
- Define governance and tracking mechanisms

### Activities

| Activity | Owner | Duration | Deliverable |
|----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Define roadmap horizons (30/60/90 day, 1yr, 3yr) | Project Lead | Day 1 | Horizon framework |
| Identify dependencies between initiatives | Technical Lead | Days 1-3 | Dependency map |
| Sequence initiatives | Project Lead | Days 3-5 | Sequenced roadmap |
| Resource and budget alignment | Analyst | Days 4-6 | Resource plan |
| Risk and contingency planning | Analyst | Days 5-7 | Risk mitigation plan |

| Build governance model | Project Lead | Days 7-9 | Governance framework |
| Create detailed 90-day action plan | Analyst | Days 8-10 | 90-day plan |

### Roadmap Horizons

**Immediate (0-90 Days):**
- Quick wins requiring minimal effort
- Low-risk retirements
- License true-ups and contract renegotiations
- Documentation and process improvements

**Short-term (3-12 Months):**
- Application retirements (complex)
- Initial consolidations
- Cloud migrations (lift-and-shift)
- Vendor negotiations

**Medium-term (1-2 Years):**
- Major consolidation projects
- Platform modernization
- Re-architecture initiatives
- Strategic vendor decisions

**Long-term (2-3 Years):**
- Full modernization programs
- Digital transformation initiatives
- Target architecture realization

### Prioritization Framework

**Value Score (40%):**
- Cost savings potential
- Risk reduction
- Strategic alignment
- User satisfaction improvement

**Feasibility Score (30%):**
- Technical complexity
- Resource availability
- Vendor/contract constraints
- Change management effort

**Urgency Score (30%):**
- End-of-support deadlines
- Security vulnerabilities
- Compliance requirements
- Business criticality
