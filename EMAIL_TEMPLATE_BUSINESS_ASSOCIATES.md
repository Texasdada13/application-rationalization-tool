## Tips for Using These Email Templates

### Personalization is Key:
1. **Replace all [bracketed] placeholders** with specific details
2. **Research the recipient** - reference their recent work, company initiatives, or industry challenges
3. **Adjust tone** based on your relationship (formal for cold outreach, casual for close contacts)

### Timing Matters:
- **Best send times**: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm local time
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, holidays
- **Follow up**: 7-10 days if no response, then again at 2-3 weeks

### Subject Line Tips:
- Keep under 50 characters
- Be specific (avoid "Opportunity" or "Quick Question")
- Create urgency or curiosity when appropriate
- Consider A/B testing different approaches

### Attachment Strategy:
- **Option 1**: Attach both the one-pager and full capability statement
- **Option 2**: Attach only the one-pager, offer full docs if interested
- **Option 3**: Don't attach anything, offer to send materials after response
- **Best practice**: Mention attachments in the email body

### Call-to-Action Best Practices:
- Be specific: "Are you available Tuesday or Wednesday next week?"
- Make it easy: Include calendar link
- Offer flexibility: "Happy to work around your schedule"
- Lower the barrier: "Just 15-20 minutes" vs. "an hour"

---

## Segmentation Strategy

**High-Priority Tier 1** (Warm Connections):
- Use Email Option 4 (Warm Introduction Request)
- Follow up within 3-5 days
- Offer to brief them before the intro

**Tier 2** (Qualified Cold Prospects):
- Use Email Option 1 (Enterprise) or 2 (Consulting)
- Personalize heavily with company research
- Follow up at 7-10 days, then 2-3 weeks

**Tier 3** (Broad Networking):
- Use Email Option 5 (LinkedIn) or 6 (Mass Outreach)
- Lower expectations, higher volume
- Focus on starting conversations, not closing deals
